Shari Liu is a cognitive scientist who studies origins of intelligence in human infants. She obtained her PhD from Harvard University in 2020, and is currently a postdoctoral fellow at MIT. She is the recipient of the Goethals Teaching Prize (2019) and Bok Center Certificate of Distinction in Teaching (2018, 2019) from Harvard University, and the Glushko Dissertation Prize  (2021) from the Cognitive Science Society. Starting in July 2023, she will be an Assistant Professor at Johns Hopkins University.
