Chapter 1: Introduction

This chapter introduces the concept of interpretable machine learning and outlines the key topics covered in the book.

Chapter 2: Linear Models

This chapter covers linear models and their interpretation, including linear regression and logistic regression.

Chapter 3: Decision Trees

This chapter covers decision trees and their interpretation, including tree-based ensemble methods.

Chapter 4: Rule-Based Models

This chapter covers rule-based models and their interpretation, including association rule mining and decision rules.

Chapter 5: Rule Ensembles

This chapter covers rule ensembles and their interpretation, including bagging, boosting, and random forests.

Chapter 6: Additive Models

This chapter covers additive models and their interpretation, including generalized additive models and tree-based additive models.

Chapter 7: Interpretable Neural Networks

This chapter covers interpretable neural networks and their interpretation, including structured neural networks and attention mechanisms.

Chapter 8: Model-Agnostic Methods

This chapter covers model-agnostic methods for interpreting machine learning models, including feature importance, partial dependence plots, and SHAP values.

Chapter 9: Post-Hoc Analysis

This chapter covers post-hoc analysis for interpreting machine learning models, including LIME and other local explanation methods.

About the Book

This book provides a comprehensive overview of interpretable machine learning methods and their applications. It is aimed at researchers, practitioners, and students who are interested in understanding and interpreting the decisions made by machine learning models.
